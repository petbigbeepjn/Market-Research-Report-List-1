<p><strong>The Artificial Hip Joint Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Metal-on-Metal</li><li>Metal-on-Polyethylene</li><li>Ceramic-on-Metal</li><li>Ceramic-on-Polyethylene</li><li>Ceramic-on-Ceramic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The artificial hip joint market comprises various implant types based on the materials used in bearing surfaces. Metal-on-metal joints feature metal heads and cups, offering durability but potential wear issues. Metal-on-polyethylene uses a metal ball with a plastic socket, striking a balance between wear resistance and cost. Ceramic-on-metal combines a ceramic ball with a metal socket, reducing friction. Ceramic-on-polyethylene uses a ceramic ball with a plastic liner for improved wear reduction, while ceramic-on-ceramic pairs two ceramic components for minimal wear and longevity.</p></p>
