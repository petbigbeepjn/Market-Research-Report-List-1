<p><p>The antidiabetic drug market encompasses various classes targeting glucose regulation. Alpha-glucosidase inhibitors slow carbohydrate absorption, while biguanides, like metformin, enhance insulin sensitivity. Sulphonylureas stimulate insulin secretion, and GLP-1 agonists promote insulin release and suppress appetite. Meglitinides offer rapid insulin release. DPP-4 inhibitors enhance incretin levels, improving blood sugar control, while SGLT-2 inhibitors prevent glucose reabsorption in kidneys. Thiazolidinediones improve insulin sensitivity in muscles and adipose tissue, collectively providing diverse therapeutic strategies for managing diabetes.</p></p>
