<p><p>The nanomembrane market is segmented into three main types: polymeric, hybrid, and inorganic membranes. Polymeric membranes are made from organic polymers and are known for their flexibility and cost-effectiveness, primarily used in water treatment and gas separation. Hybrid membranes combine different materials to enhance performance, offering a balance of mechanical strength and permeability. Inorganic membranes, composed of materials like ceramics or metals, provide superior thermal and chemical resistance, making them suitable for high-temperature and aggressive environments.</p></p>
