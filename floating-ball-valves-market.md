<p><p>Floating ball valves are a type of quarter-turn valve that utilizes a spherical disc, or "ball," to control the flow of fluid. The ball is positioned within the valve body and is held in place by seat sealing surfaces on either side. When the valve is open, the ball's hole aligns with the flow path; when closed, the ball rotates to block the flow, providing a reliable shut-off mechanism.</p><p>The Floating Ball Valves Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.1% during the forecast period, driven by rising demand across various sectors such as oil and gas, water treatment, and chemical processing. Increasing infrastructure investments and the necessity for efficient fluid control systems contribute to this growth. Additionally, the trend towards automation and smart technologies in valve systems is reshaping market dynamics. Enhanced durability and resistance to high pressures are propelling innovations, with manufacturers focusing on advanced materials and design improvements. <p><p>The floating ball valves market is segmented into three main types: manual, electric, and pneumatic. Manual floating ball valves are operated by hand, providing simple and reliable control for various applications. Electric floating ball valves utilize electric actuators for automated operation, enhancing efficiency and precision in fluid control systems. Pneumatic floating ball valves rely on compressed air to operate, offering rapid action and responsiveness, making them ideal for processes requiring quick shut-off or flow regulation in industrial settings.</p></p>
