<p><p>The peanut allergy treatment market is divided into oral and injectable therapies. Oral treatments involve administering gradually increasing amounts of peanut protein to build tolerance, often through capsules or food products. Injectable treatments typically include epinephrine auto-injectors, used for emergency management of severe allergic reactions. Both methods aim to mitigate the risk of anaphylaxis, with oral therapies focusing on long-term desensitization, while injectables provide immediate relief during allergic emergencies. Each approach addresses peanut allergies' significant health impact.</p></p>
