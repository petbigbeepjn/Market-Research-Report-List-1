<p><p>The marble facade cladding market comprises two primary types: engineered and natural. Engineered marble is a composite material made from crushed marble and resin, offering consistent quality, diverse colors, and enhanced durability. In contrast, natural marble is quarried stone known for its unique patterns and textures, providing a classic, luxurious aesthetic. Both types serve architectural needs, with engineered marble being popular for modern constructions due to its practicality, while natural marble appeals to those seeking timeless elegance and authenticity.</p></p>
