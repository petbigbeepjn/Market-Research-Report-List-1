<p><p>The biofertilizers market comprises several key types, each contributing to sustainable agriculture. Azotobacter and Azospirillum are free-living nitrogen-fixing bacteria that improve soil fertility. Rhizobium forms symbiotic relationships with legumes, enhancing nitrogen availability. Phosphate-solubilizing bacteria help convert insoluble phosphates into accessible forms for plants. Cyanobacteria, also known as blue-green algae, fix atmospheric nitrogen and improve soil health. Additionally, other specialized microbial formulations cater to diverse agricultural needs, promoting organic farming and reducing chemical fertilizer reliance.</p></p>
