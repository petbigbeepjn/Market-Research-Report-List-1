<p><p>The hand-held thermal imager market is primarily segmented into two types: uncooled and cooled thermal imagers. Uncooled hand-held thermal imagers operate at ambient temperatures, utilizing materials like microbolometers, making them compact, lightweight, and cost-effective, ideal for general inspections and basic applications. In contrast, cooled hand-held thermal imagers use cryogenic cooling systems to enhance sensitivity and image quality, making them suited for specialized tasks in defense, industrial inspections, and high-end research, but at a higher price point.</p></p>
