<p><p>The intracranial support catheter market comprises two main types: adjustable and non-adjustable catheters. Adjustable catheters allow for customization of pressure settings based on patient needs, providing improved management of intracranial pressure. In contrast, non-adjustable catheters offer a fixed design, delivering consistent performance but limited flexibility in treatment. Both types serve critical functions in neurosurgery, assisting in the drainage of cerebrospinal fluid and managing conditions such as hydrocephalus or traumatic brain injuries, ultimately aiming to enhance patient outcomes.</p></p>
