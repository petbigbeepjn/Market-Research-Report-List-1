<p><p>Chemotherapy-Induced Nausea and Vomiting (CINV) drugs are categorized based on the timing and severity of symptoms. Acute CINV occurs within 24 hours post-treatment, requiring fast-acting antiemetics. Delayed CINV arises after the first day and may last several days, necessitating longer-lasting medications. Breakthrough CINV refers to symptoms that occur despite prophylactic treatment, requiring rescue therapies. Other CINV applications encompass anticipatory nausea and vomiting associated with chemotherapy. Each category emphasizes tailored approaches for effective symptom management.</p></p>
