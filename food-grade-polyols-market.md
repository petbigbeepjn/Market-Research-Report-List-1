<p><p>The food grade polyols market comprises several key types, including sorbitol, glycerol, propylene glycol, acetoglycerides, and others. Sorbitol is commonly used as a sugar substitute and sweetener. Glycerol acts as a humectant and stabilizer, enhancing moisture retention. Propylene glycol serves as a solvent and flavoring agent. Acetoglycerides function as emulsifiers, improving texture and consistency in food products. Other variants include mannitol and erythritol, which also contribute to the formulation of low-calorie and functional food items.</p></p>
