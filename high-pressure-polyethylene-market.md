<p><p>The High-Pressure Polyethylene market includes two primary production methods: the Autoclave Method and the Tube Method. The Autoclave Method uses a high-pressure reactor to polymerize ethylene, producing polyethylene with a highly branched structure, ideal for applications requiring flexibility and clarity. The Tube Method employs tubular reactors, offering better temperature control and producing polyethylene with a more uniform molecular structure, suitable for high-strength applications. Both methods cater to diverse industries, with the choice depending on desired product properties and production efficiency.</p></p>
