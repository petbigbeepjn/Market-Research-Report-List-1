<p><p>Self-expanding peripheral vascular stents are medical devices designed to support the walls of peripheral blood vessels, ensuring patency and preventing re-narrowing after procedures such as angioplasty. These stents expand automatically once positioned in the desired location, adapting to the vessel's diameter. They are primarily used in treating peripheral artery disease (PAD), which affects blood flow in the extremities.</p><p>The market for self-expanding peripheral vascular stents is experiencing significant growth, driven by an aging population, increasing prevalence of chronic diseases, and growing awareness of minimally invasive surgical procedures.
