<p><p>The Near Infrared Medical Imaging market is primarily divided into two types: Near Infrared Fluorescence Imaging Devices and Near Infrared Bioluminescence Imaging Devices. Near Infrared Fluorescence Imaging Devices utilize fluorescent dyes that emit light when stimulated, allowing for real-time visualization of biological processes and tumor localization. Conversely, Near Infrared Bioluminescence Imaging Devices leverage natural luminescent proteins to provide high-resolution images of cellular activity, facilitating research in oncology and drug development. Both technologies enhance diagnostic accuracy and therapeutic monitoring.</p></p>
