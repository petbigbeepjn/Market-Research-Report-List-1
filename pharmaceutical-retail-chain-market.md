<p><p>Pharmaceutical retail chains can be categorized into three main types: </p><p>1. **Regular Chain (RC)**: These are corporate-owned stores operating under a unified brand, ensuring consistent product offerings and pricing across locations.</p><p>2. **Franchise Chain (FC)**: Operated by independent franchisees, these stores leverage a recognized brand while maintaining some operational autonomy, facilitating local ownership and management.</p><p>3. **Voluntary Chain (VC)**: This model consists of independently owned pharmacies that collaborate for bulk purchasing and marketing benefits without losing their operational independence, promoting competitive pricing and shared resources.</p></p>
