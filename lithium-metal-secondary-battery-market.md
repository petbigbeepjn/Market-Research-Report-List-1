Conversely, Li/Sulfur batteries leverage the high theoretical capacity of sulfur, providing a lightweight and cost-effective alternative, although challenges like limited cycling stability and efficiency remain. Both types are pivotal in advancing energy storage technologies, catering to electric vehicles and renewable energy systems.</p></p>
