<p><p>The aerospace carbon fiber market is primarily segmented into polyacrylonitrile (PAN)-based and pitch-based carbon fibers. PAN-based carbon fibers are widely used due to their superior strength-to-weight ratio and thermal stability, making them ideal for aircraft structures and components. In contrast, pitch-based carbon fibers, known for their high thermal conductivity and modulus, are utilized in specialized applications like thermal protection systems. Both types contribute significantly to enhancing fuel efficiency and performance in the aerospace industry, driving innovation and material advancements.</p></p>
