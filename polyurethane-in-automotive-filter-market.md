<p><p>Polyurethane in the automotive filter market primarily includes one-component and two-component types. One-component polyurethane is ready-to-use, allowing for efficient application with minimal mixing, making it suitable for simpler filter designs. In contrast, two-component polyurethane involves mixing a resin and a hardener, providing enhanced durability and performance, making it ideal for high-stress filters. Both types are valued for their excellent filtration properties, resistance to harsh conditions, and ability to enhance the longevity of automotive components in various environments.</p></p>
