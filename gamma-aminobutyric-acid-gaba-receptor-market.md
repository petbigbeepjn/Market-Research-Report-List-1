<p><p>The GABA receptor market is primarily categorized into two types: GABA-A and GABA-B receptors. GABA-A receptors are ionotropic receptors that mediate fast inhibitory neurotransmission and are commonly targeted in treatments for anxiety, epilepsy, and sleep disorders. GABA-B receptors, on the other hand, are metabotropic receptors that modulate slower inhibitory responses and are involved in muscle relaxation and pain management. The distinction between these receptor types influences research, drug development, and therapeutic applications in neurological and psychiatric conditions.</p></p>
